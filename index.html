    Yixuan Chen, Yubin Shi, Mingzhi Dong, Xiaochen Yang, Dongsheng Li, Yujiang Wang, Robert P. Dick, Qin Lv, Yingying Zhao, Fan Yang, and Li Shang.

We theoretically analyze that convergence and generalization abilities of over-parameterized models can be upper-bounded by the ratio of the Lipschitz constant and the Polyak-Łojasiewicz (PL) constant (the condition number). Based on this, we propose a structured pruning method with a gating network that dynamically detects and masks out poorly-behaved nodes during training.

Research Experience

EchoSage: A Unified LLM for Cardiologist-level Echocardiography

Goal: Building a unified large language model that perceives, reasons over, and reports on echocardiograms like a human clinician, moving beyond fixed diagnostic labels.

  • Developed EchoSage, a unified vision-language LLM trained on 586,903 studies that jointly perceives multi-view, multi-modal ultrasound, reasons holistically, and synthesises findings in natural language.
  • Enabled comprehensive diagnosis of 64 cardiovascular findings and diseases with transparent, interactive clinical reasoning.
  • Evaluated on 19,977 studies from four hospitals, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art generic and medical LLMs; its vision encoder also serves as a strong diagnostic foundation model.
  • Achieved echocardiographic assessment comparable to advanced echocardiographers (Level II competency), as the first conversational AI with echocardiologist-level competency.

Machine Unlearning in Healthcare

Goal: Enable data removal from medical AI models to comply with privacy regulations while maintaining algorithmic fairness.

  • Identified an ethical dilemma in clinical AI: protecting patients' right to be forgotten compromises honest decision-making for remaining populations.
  • Developed a fair unlearning framework that effectively removes medical records from trained models while mitigating algorithmic bias and preserving demographic equity.
  • Validated on multiple real-world datasets for multiple medical tasks, demonstrating effective record removal while reducing demographic unfairness.

Over-parameterized Model Optimization

Goal: Enhancing training efficiency and generalization capabilities of over-parameterized neural networks.

  • Established theoretical foundations by proving that convergence and generalization bounds are determined by the ratio of Lipschitz and PL constants (condition number).
  • Developed an adaptive structured pruning method with a gating network to dynamically suppress parameters with high condition numbers during training.
  • Demonstrated improvements in training efficiency and model performance across three distinct neural architectures.

Unsupervised Disentangled Representation Learning

Goal: Advancing disentangled representation learning in compositional feature spaces.

  • Formulated an information-theoretic framework for disentanglement learning in compositional feature spaces.
  • Designed a recursive disentanglement network that propagates regulatory inductive bias across the compositional feature hierarchy.
  • Conducted experiments on three datasets to demonstrate its effectiveness in disentanglement quality.

Multi-modal Representation Learning

Goal: Developing robust multi-modal representations (especially for fake news detection).

  • Pioneered an information-theoretic approach (KL Divergence) to quantify the cross-modal ambiguity in multi-modal learning.
  • Implemented an ambiguity-aware detection framework that adaptively integrates unimodal features and cross-modal correlations based on confidence measures.
  • Achieved improved classification performance on two benchmark datasets.
